## Ethics-First Design

PhotoBoard is built on the principle that **participatory research must center participant safety, dignity, and autonomy**. Before any photograph can be captured, the app enforces a comprehensive ethical consent process that educates participants and obtains informed agreement.

<Warning>
  PhotoVoice research involves documenting sensitive social realities. Without robust ethical protocols, participants can face risks including:
  - Privacy violations if identities are exposed
  - Physical safety threats when documenting injustice
  - Psychological harm from reliving trauma
  - Legal consequences in contexts of criminalized activities
</Warning>

## Ethical Consent Workflow

PhotoBoard implements mandatory informed consent before camera access. This is not just a legal checkbox - it's an educational moment about participatory research ethics.

### Implementation

The consent dialog appears every time a participant initiates photo capture:

```dart
// home_screen.dart:17-77
Future<void> _openCamera(BuildContext context) async {
  // 1. Show Ethical Consent screen/dialog
  final bool? accepted = await showDialog<bool>(
    context: context,
    barrierDismissible: false, // Cannot be dismissed without decision
    builder: (BuildContext dialogContext) {
      return AlertDialog(
        backgroundColor: const Color(0xFF1E1E1E),
        shape: RoundedRectangleBorder(borderRadius: BorderRadius.circular(20)),
        title: const Text(
          'Consentimiento Ético',
          style: TextStyle(color: Colors.white, fontWeight: FontWeight.bold),
        ),
```

<Note>
  **Key Design Decision**: `barrierDismissible: false` means participants cannot accidentally bypass consent - they must explicitly choose "Acepto" (I accept) or "No acepto" (I don't accept).
</Note>

### The Three Core Ethical Principles

PhotoBoard's consent dialog presents three fundamental commitments:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Privacy & Consent">
    ```dart
    // home_screen.dart:34-40
    ListTile(
      leading: Icon(Icons.privacy_tip, color: Colors.blueAccent),
      title: Text(
        'Respeto la privacidad y no fotografiaré rostros sin '
        'consentimiento explícito.',
        style: TextStyle(color: Colors.white70, fontSize: 15),
      ),
    ),
    ```
    
    **Translation**: "I respect privacy and will not photograph faces without explicit consent."
    
    **Why it matters**: Many PhotoVoice projects involve documenting community members. This principle requires participants to:
    - Ask permission before photographing identifiable individuals
    - Explain how the image will be used
    - Obtain verbal or written consent
    - Respect refusals without pressure
    
    <Tip>
      **Best Practice**: Provide participants with consent forms they can have community members sign if faces are included. Store these separately from the PhotoBoard database.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Physical Safety">
    ```dart
    // home_screen.dart:41-47
    ListTile(
      leading: Icon(Icons.health_and_safety, color: Colors.orangeAccent),
      title: Text(
        'Priorizo mi seguridad física y la de otros al tomar '
        'fotos en el entorno.',
        style: TextStyle(color: Colors.white70, fontSize: 15),
      ),
    ),
    ```
    
    **Translation**: "I prioritize my physical safety and that of others when taking photos in the environment."
    
    **Why it matters**: Documenting social issues can expose participants to risk:
    - Entering unsafe physical spaces (abandoned buildings, high-traffic roads)
    - Photographing illegal activities or confrontational subjects
    - Drawing attention in contexts of surveillance or police presence
    - Weather hazards, environmental toxins, or structural dangers
    
    <Warning>
      **Risk Assessment**: Before launching a PhotoVoice project, conduct a safety analysis with community partners. Identify no-go zones, times of day to avoid, and protocols if participants feel threatened.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Purpose & Use">
    ```dart
    // home_screen.dart:48-54
    ListTile(
      leading: Icon(Icons.public, color: Colors.greenAccent),
      title: Text(
        'Entiendo que mis fotos y narrativas se usarán para '
        'visibilizar realidades sociales y generar diálogo.',
        style: TextStyle(color: Colors.white70, fontSize: 15),
      ),
    ),
    ```
    
    **Translation**: "I understand that my photos and narratives will be used to make social realities visible and generate dialogue."
    
    **Why it matters**: This establishes:
    - **Informed consent**: Participants know their contributions are research data
    - **Purpose limitation**: Images are for social advocacy, not commercial use
    - **Public nature**: Content may be shared in community forums, presentations, or policy meetings
    - **Agency**: Participants are co-creators of knowledge, not research subjects
    
    <Info>
      This principle is grounded in participatory action research ethics - research *with* communities, not *on* communities.
    </Info>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Consent Decision Flow

After reading the three principles, participants make a binary choice:

```dart
// home_screen.dart:58-74
actions: [
  TextButton(
    onPressed: () => Navigator.pop(dialogContext, false),
    child: const Text('No acepto', style: TextStyle(color: Colors.grey)),
  ),
  ElevatedButton(
    onPressed: () => Navigator.pop(dialogContext, true),
    style: ElevatedButton.styleFrom(
      backgroundColor: const Color(0xFF8B5CF6),
      shape: RoundedRectangleBorder(borderRadius: BorderRadius.circular(10)),
    ),
    child: const Text('Acepto y Comprendo', 
                      style: TextStyle(color: Colors.white)),
  ),
],
```

**"No acepto"** (I don't accept):
- Returns `false` and aborts the photo capture workflow
- No camera access granted
- Participant returns to home screen

**"Acepto y Comprendo"** (I accept and understand):
- Returns `true` and proceeds to camera
- Confirms participant has read and understood ethical commitments

<Note>
  The button text is "I accept **and understand**" - emphasizing comprehension, not just agreement. This aligns with informed consent standards in research ethics.
</Note>

```dart
// home_screen.dart:76-77
// If user didn't accept or closed dialog, abort the flow
if (accepted != true) return;
```

## Privacy Protections

Beyond the consent workflow, PhotoBoard implements several privacy-by-design features:

### Anonymous Participation

All posts are displayed as "Participante Anónimo" (Anonymous Participant):

```dart
// post_card.dart:28-46
ListTile(
  contentPadding: const EdgeInsets.symmetric(horizontal: 16, vertical: 4),
  leading: CircleAvatar(
    radius: 16,
    backgroundColor: Theme.of(context).colorScheme.primary.withOpacity(0.2),
    child: Icon(
      Icons.person_outline,
      size: 20,
      color: Theme.of(context).colorScheme.primary,
    ),
  ),
  title: const Text(
    'Participante Anónimo', // Protected identity, centering the voice
    style: TextStyle(
      fontWeight: FontWeight.w600,
      fontSize: 14,
      color: Colors.white,
    ),
  ),
```

<Tip>
  **Design Rationale**: PhotoVoice research centers **message over messenger**. Anonymity prevents bias, protects vulnerable participants, and shifts focus to the social issues being documented.
</Tip>

### Data Minimization

The Post model stores only essential data:

```dart
// post.dart:1-12
class Post {
  final String id;
  final String imageUrl;
  final String comment;
  final DateTime createdAt;
  
  Post({
    required this.id,
    required this.imageUrl,
    required this.comment,
    required this.createdAt,
  });
```

**What's NOT collected**:
- User names or profiles
- Device identifiers
- Location metadata
- Contact information
- IP addresses

<Info>
  This aligns with GDPR principles of data minimization and purpose limitation - only collect what's necessary for the research purpose.
</Info>

### Secure Storage

Images and data are stored in Supabase with configurable access controls:

```dart
// post_repository.dart:12-24
Future<String> uploadImage(File file, String fileName) async {
  final storagePath = 'public/$fileName';
  
  // Upload file to "photos" bucket
  await _supabase.storage.from('photos').upload(storagePath, file);
  
  // Get public URL to save in database
  final String publicUrl = _supabase.storage
      .from('photos')
      .getPublicUrl(storagePath);
      
  return publicUrl;
}
```

<Warning>
  **Implementation Note**: The code shows `public/$fileName` - for sensitive research, configure the Supabase bucket as **private** and use signed URLs with expiration times instead of public URLs.
</Warning>

## Export Ethics

PhotoBoard includes data export functionality for researchers, which raises additional ethical considerations:

```dart
// home_screen.dart:95-146
Future<void> _exportData(BuildContext context) async {
  // ...
  List<List<dynamic>> csvData = [
    ['Fecha', 'URL_Imagen', 'Narrativa_Reflexion', 'ID_Participante'],
  ];
  
  for (var post in posts) {
    csvData.add([
      post.createdAt.toIso8601String(),
      post.imageUrl,
      post.comment,
      post.id,
    ]);
  }
  
  await Share.shareXFiles(
    [XFile(path)],
    text: 'Exportación de datos de codificación cualitativa - PhotoVoice',
  );
}
```

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Who can export data?" icon="user-shield">
    **Current implementation**: Any user with access to the home screen can export all data (home_screen.dart:173-177).
    
    **Best practice recommendation**: Restrict export functionality to:
    - Authenticated researcher accounts
    - Project facilitators with approved IRB protocols
    - Community advisory board members
    
    **Implementation approach**:
    ```dart
    // Add role-based access control
    if (currentUser.role == 'researcher' || currentUser.role == 'facilitator') {
      IconButton(
        icon: const Icon(Icons.download_rounded),
        onPressed: () => _exportData(context),
      )
    }
    ```
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What data is exported?" icon="database">
    The CSV includes:
    - Timestamp (for temporal analysis)
    - Image URL (for visual analysis)
    - Full narrative text (for qualitative coding)
    - Post ID (for database linkage)
    
    **Ethical consideration**: The exported data contains participant narratives that may include identifying information or sensitive disclosures.
    
    <Warning>
      Researchers must:
      - Store exported files securely (encrypted drives)
      - Remove identifying information during analysis
      - Delete files after research completion per IRB protocols
      - Never share raw data publicly
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How should exported data be used?" icon="gavel">
    The export is labeled "Exportación de datos de codificación cualitativa - PhotoVoice" (Export of qualitative coding data - PhotoVoice).
    
    **Appropriate uses**:
    - Thematic analysis in qualitative software (NVivo, Atlas.ti, etc.)
    - Developing codebooks for narrative analysis
    - Creating anonymized excerpts for research papers
    - Identifying patterns for community presentations
    
    **Inappropriate uses**:
    - Publishing raw narratives with participant identification
    - Sharing images without community approval
    - Using data for purposes not disclosed in consent
    - Commercial applications
    
    <Tip>
      Best practice: Bring analysis back to the participant community for validation and interpretation before external dissemination.
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices for Facilitators

If you're implementing PhotoBoard for a participatory research project:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Develop a research protocol">
    Before deployment:
    - Submit to Institutional Review Board (IRB) or ethics committee
    - Create detailed consent forms (beyond the in-app consent)
    - Develop risk mitigation strategies
    - Plan data security and retention policies
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Conduct orientation sessions">
    Train participants on:
    - The three ethical principles in depth
    - How to obtain consent from community members photographed
    - Safety protocols specific to your research context
    - What will happen to their photos and narratives
    - Their rights (withdrawal, data deletion, anonymity)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Establish community agreements">
    Collaboratively develop:
    - Guidelines for sensitive topics (when NOT to photograph)
    - Decision-making processes for data use
    - Protocols for handling risky situations
    - Collective ownership of research outputs
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Implement technical safeguards">
    Configure PhotoBoard securely:
    - Use private Supabase storage buckets
    - Implement authentication for export functionality
    - Set up data encryption
    - Create backup systems with access controls
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor ongoing ethics">
    Throughout the research:
    - Check in regularly about participant wellbeing
    - Adjust protocols if new risks emerge
    - Ensure participants can withdraw without penalty
    - Maintain transparent communication about data use
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Share ownership of outcomes">
    At project conclusion:
    - Co-present findings with participants
    - Get community approval before external dissemination
    - Ensure research benefits the community
    - Delete data per agreed retention schedules
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Ethical Tensions in Participatory Research

PhotoVoice methodology involves inherent ethical complexities:

<Accordion title="Balancing visibility and safety">
  **The tension**: PhotoVoice aims to make invisible issues visible, but visibility can expose participants to risk.
  
  **Navigation strategies**:
  - Use anonymization techniques (face blurring, voice distortion for presentations)
  - Let participants choose which images are shared publicly vs. kept internal
  - Create multiple versions of outputs (community version vs. policy version)
  - Frame issues collectively rather than as individual stories
  
  <Info>
    Example: A participant documents police harassment. The image is used in community discussions but not shared with media until the group strategically decides to do so with legal support.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Individual consent vs. collective benefit">
  **The tension**: Participatory research is meant to benefit the community, but individuals bear the burden of participation.
  
  **Navigation strategies**:
  - Compensate participants for time and expertise
  - Build in collective benefit mechanisms (community events, resource allocation)
  - Ensure participants have agency over individual data even if collective project continues
  - Create shared ownership structures for any products (reports, exhibits, grants)
  
  <Tip>
    Some projects establish community funds where any revenue from PhotoVoice outputs returns to the community for self-determined purposes.
  </Tip>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Researcher needs vs. participant control">
  **The tension**: Academic researchers need data for publications/tenure; participants may want to control narratives.
  
  **Navigation strategies**:
  - Negotiate data use agreements upfront
  - Use co-authorship models
  - Share draft publications with participants for feedback
  - Prioritize community dissemination (local events, policy briefs) before academic outputs
  - Consider community intellectual property rights
  
  <Warning>
    Extractive research (take data, leave community) violates PhotoVoice ethics. If there's a conflict between researcher career needs and community control, community should prevail.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

## Continuous Consent

PhotoBoard's **per-capture consent** (requiring agreement each time the camera is opened) is a form of continuous consent:

```dart
// This runs every time the camera button is pressed
Future<void> _openCamera(BuildContext context) async {
  final bool? accepted = await showDialog<bool>(...);
  if (accepted != true) return;
  // ...
}
```

**Advantages**:
- Participants re-affirm ethical commitments each session
- Prevents habituation (going through motions without reflection)
- Allows participants to opt out at any point
- Creates moments for re-assessing safety

**Consideration**:
- May feel repetitive for experienced participants
- Could be complemented with a "remember my choice for this session" option

<Tip>
  For long-term projects (months/years), consider periodic re-consent sessions where participants can update their preferences and facilitators can address emerging ethical questions.
</Tip>
